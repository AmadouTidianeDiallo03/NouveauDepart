# The Quebec University System

**Audience:** International students  
**Updated:** 2024

---

## 1. Structure of the Quebec University System

Quebec has a different higher education structure from the rest of Canada and from most countries:

- **CEGEP** (Collège d'enseignement général et professionnel): 2-year pre-university college, mandatory for students from Quebec secondary school
- **Bachelor's (Baccalauréat)**: 3 years / 90 credits – equivalent to a Bachelor's degree
- **Master's (Maîtrise)**: 2 years after the Bachelor's
- **PhD (Doctorat)**: 4–6 years

⚠️ Students with a French "Licence" (180 ECTS) or equivalent may be eligible for direct entry into a Master's program. Confirm with the registrar's office.

---

## 2. University Credits

- A standard course = **3 credits**
- Bachelor's degree = **90 credits** (30 per year)
- Full-time students take **4–5 courses per semester** (12–15 credits)
- Credits are non-refundable after the refund deadline

---

## 3. Academic Semesters

- **Fall (Automne)**: early September → late December (exams in December)
- **Winter (Hiver)**: early January → late April (exams in April)
- **Summer (Été)** (optional): May → late August (fewer courses available)

Course registration is done online through the student portal, several weeks before the start of each semester.

---

## 4. Grades and Evaluation

**Typical grading scale:**

| Grade | Letter | Meaning |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 90-100% | A+ | Excellent |
| 85-89% | A | Very Good |
| 80-84% | A- | Very Good |
| 75-79% | B+ | Good |
| 70-74% | B | Good |
| 65-69% | C+ | Acceptable |
| 60-64% | C | Passing grade |
| < 60% | E/F | Failure |

**Typical grade composition:**
- Assignments / exercises: 30–40%
- Midterm exam: 20–30%
- Final exam: 30–40%

---

## 5. Course Outline (Syllabus / Plan de cours)

The course outline is an **essential document** given on the first class. It contains:
- Course objectives
- Session schedule
- Evaluation methods and weights
- Attendance and submission rules
- Academic integrity policy

**Read it carefully from day one.**

---

## 6. Student ID and Permanent Code

- **Student ID (matricule)**: your unique university identifier
- **Permanent code** (e.g., TREM12345678): assigned by Quebec's Ministry of Education, follows you lifelong in the province
- Required for course registration, exams, and transcript requests

---

## 7. Official Transcript

- Available via the student portal or at the registrar's office
- Two versions: **Official** (sealed, fee-based) and **Unofficial** (free, for personal use)
- Processing time: 5–10 business days for official transcripts

---

## 8. Academic Integrity and Plagiarism

Plagiarism is a serious academic offence.

**Forms of plagiarism:**
- Copy-pasting text without quotation marks or citation
- Paraphrasing without citing the source
- Submitting someone else's work as your own
- Using generative AI without explicit instructor permission

**Consequences:** Zero on the assignment, course failure, or even suspension.

**How to cite correctly:** Ask your university which citation style is required (APA, Chicago, MLA).

---

## 9. Academic Support Services

- **Registrar's office (Registrariat)**: registration, transcripts, admission
- **Writing centre / tutors**: help with written assignments
- **Library (Bibliothèque)**: online resources (Érudit, ProQuest, REPÈRES), study rooms
- **Student accessibility services**: exam accommodations
- **Academic advisor (Conseiller pédagogique)**: help choosing courses and planning your path
