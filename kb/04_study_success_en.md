# Academic Success in Quebec

**Audience:** International students  
**Updated:** 2024

---

## 1. Effective Study Techniques

### Pomodoro Technique
1. Study for 25 minutes without interruption
2. Take a 5-minute break
3. Repeat 4 times
4. Take a long break (15–30 min)

### Cornell Note-Taking Method
Divide your page into two columns:
- **Left column (narrow)**: key questions and keywords
- **Right column (wide)**: detailed class notes
- **Bottom area**: 2–3 sentence summary written after class

### Mind Maps
Useful for courses with many interconnected concepts (history, biology, management).

---

## 2. Time Management

- **Create a calendar** from week one with all exam and submission dates
- Use **Google Calendar** or a paper planner
- Review notes **regularly** (30 min/day) rather than cramming the night before
- Expect **2 hours of outside work per hour of class** for readings and assignments

---

## 3. How to Succeed at Exams

- Get **past exams** from your student association or documentation centre
- Do **practice questions** and simulate exam conditions
- **Sleep well** the night before – memory consolidates during sleep
- Arrive **early** with all permitted materials

---

## 4. Academic Writing

**Typical structure of a university paper:**
1. Title page (per your university's format)
2. Introduction (issue statement, outline)
3. Body (arguments with citations)
4. Conclusion (synthesis, opening thought)
5. Bibliography (APA, Chicago, or MLA)

**APA Citation examples:**
- Book: Author, A. (2020). *Book title*. Publisher.
- Article: Author, A. (2020). Article title. *Journal Name*, *12*(3), 45–67.
- Website: Author. (2020, March 3). Title. URL

---

## 5. Available Academic Resources

### University Library
- Access to thousands of articles via **Érudit** (French-language) and **ProQuest**, **EBSCO**, **JSTOR**
- Silent study rooms and group study spaces
- Interlibrary loan service

### Writing Centre (Centre d'aide à la rédaction)
- Free for all students
- Help with structure, grammar, citations
- Walk-in or by appointment

### Peer Tutors
- Advanced students helping first-year peers
- Available for most introductory courses

---

## 6. Mental Health and Well-being

- Academic stress is normal but shouldn't become chronic
- **Free psychological services** are available at all Quebec universities
- Crisis line: 1-866-APPELLE (277-3553) – available 24/7

**Don't ignore these signs:**
- More than 2 weeks of poor sleep
- Paralyzing anxiety before exams
- Feeling out of place (Impostor Syndrome – very common)

---

## 7. Asking for Help

**Don't wait until you're failing to ask for help!**

- Your **professor**: during office hours – check the course outline
- Your **teaching assistant (TA)**: often more accessible
- Your **academic advisor**: program questions, course changes
- Your **NordikAventure mentor**: practical student life experience
